Educational Background

I graduated from Tom C. Clark Highschool in San Antonio, TX. I went to the University of Texas at San Antonio where I took out loans to attend my first semester of college for Computer Engineering. I quickly learned I was in debt and I was not totally devoted to my major. I hit a bit of a crisis mode when the first semester had ended and I was more lost with what my future would hold. I talked to my parents about some sort of plan - I didn’t want to give up on something I started but I also knew I would just continue to rack up debt if I continued to go to college because my parents couldn’t afford to support my college funds. My dad reassured me that I didn’t need to go to college and that was okay. He introduced me to his field of expertise and said I didn’t have to rack up debt to be successful in the tech world. I quickly fell in love with coding and the idea of being able to create a plethora of things from simply coding. My dreams finally opened up and my plan for the future finally seemed to be in order.

Family Background

I grew up the youngest out of four kids. I take a lot of pride in my family background because the person I am today has a lot to do with the way I grew up. Growing up the youngest forced me to be very cooperative with others - hence having to learn to share with three other kids. I had the opportunity to watch each of my siblings grow up and evolve to who they are today and in return I was able to see what kind of person I aspired to be. Meanwhile, my parents were really hard workers in order to support us as a family. I look up to my dad because he is a self taught software engineer. Growing up, he would constantly be studying for the next certification to wrap under his belt in order to continually strive for the next job. I look up to my mom because she supported her own family growing up as a kid. She taught us how to look out for one another and how to push through adversity and self doubt. She was my rock through my adolescence she kept my afloat and grounded to the reality of life and what actually matters. I moved out of my house and started to support myself during the pandemic where I moved to Austin, TX away from my home town and parents with my boyfriend, Will. We both push each other to keep working hard and strive to reach our career goals together.
